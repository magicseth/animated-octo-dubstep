    THE BE BORN METHOD

    • explore

      People have different needs during childbirth. Through our in-depth prenatal discussions we will explore what is most important to you during the birth of your baby. We will cover clinical options and tradeoffs, and birthing techniques. By the time you're in labor, you will know more about your options and potential choices. I will know how to best support your birth. My role encompasses the non-clinical aspects of care during childbirth.

      No birthing person, baby, partner, or birth is the same. I’m available for all kinds: multiples, LGBTQ, hospital and homebirths, labors that involve medication as well as unmedicated births. I provide reassurance and perspective to both you and your partner. I can offer comfort techniques including touch, breathwork, heat, acupressure, positioning, and aromatherapy. That said, you already know how to birth your baby. I just help you relax.

    • inform

      As a student midwife with the National Midwifery Institute, I have a good knowledge and awareness of how to protect normal birth, but I am not yet licensed to support people in a medical role. Many people consider doulas to be a must for those giving birth in a hospital, due to unnecessary inductions and interventions. I help you prepare prenatally for the reality of hospital birth in the Bay Area and I help you navigate protocols to birth with autonomy. Birth can have long term emotional and physical effects on birthing people, partners, and babies. I help you keep birth normal and empowering.

      Should your birth become more complex and require medical solutions, including a cesarean section, I will remain with you if I am allowed and help in any way I can. I will assist you through the decision making process, helping you get the information you need to make your own conscious choices. I hold the space for you and your family to decide.

    • connect

      Beyond providing emotional support and physical comfort, I facilitate communication with the medical staff to make sure you and your partner have the information necessary to make informed choices during the birth of your baby. Rather than advocating for you, I provide information prenatally about current peer-reviewed scientific journal articles on common birth choices, enabling you to advocate for yourself. This allows you to make choices that you understand and feel good about in the long run.

      My support adjusts to labor’s unpredictable nature. This model increases the opportunity for self-empowerment, informed decision making, and staying aligned with what each family holds dear while growing.

    EXPECTATIONS

    • the visits

      My fee is $850. This includes 2 prenatal home visits, support during your birth, and a postpartum visit. A discount is available for homebirth.

      Preliminary Visit: The doula and family relationship is just that, a relationship. So it is crucial that we feel like we're the right fit. This short visit is to discuss your expectations of a doula, and to see if we're a good match. We can meet in your home or a coffee shop, whichever space makes you most comfortable. I will give you my contract.

      2 Prenatal Home Visits: We'll explore your birth, your hopes, and your fears. I'll bring out my rebozo and we'll practice positions to find what feels good. We will discuss signs of labor and when you would like me to join you. I'll also answer any questions you have about labor, including potential medical solutions to bring on labor or alleviate discomfort.

      Labor and Birth: I'll give you both support and space in turn.

      Postpartum Home Visit: We will talk about your birth experience and celebrate you, your baby, and your growing family. I'll also address any concerns you have and give referrals as necessary.
