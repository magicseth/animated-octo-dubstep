    THE BE BORN METHOD

    • explore

      Women have different needs during childbirth. Through our in-depth prenatal discussions we will explore what is most important to you during the birth of your baby. In addition to the safety of medical care and the love and companionship provided by their partners, women need continuous reassurance, comfort, encouragement, and respect. They need individualized care based on their unique circumstances and preferences. My role encompasses the non-clinical aspects of care during childbirth.

      No momma, baby, partner, or birth is the same. I’m available for all kinds: multiples, LGBT, hospital and homebirths, labors that involve medication and interventions, as well as natural unmedicated births. I provide reassurance and perspective to both you and your partner, making gentle suggestions to nudge labor along, and assisting with comfort techniques including relaxation, massage, positioning, and touch.

    • inform

      As an aspiring midwife, I have a good knowledge and awareness of female physiology, but I do not yet support mommas in a medical role – that is the job of the midwife or doctor. Many women consider doulas to be a must for those giving birth in a hospital, due to unnecessary inductions and interventions. Birth can have long term emotional and physical effects on mommas, partners, and babies. I help you keep birth normal and empowering.

      Should your birth become more complex and require medical assistance, including a cesarean section, I will remain with you and help in any way I can. I will assist you through the decision making process, providing balanced information so you and your partner can make your own conscious choices. I can help you advocate for yourself, leading you away from a disempowering, disappointing experience. I hold the space for you and your family to decide.

    • connect

      Beyond providing emotional support and physical comfort, I facilitate communication with the medical staff to make sure mommas and their partners have the information necessary to make informed choices during the birth of their baby. Rather than advocating for you, I provide information, guidance and support, enabling you to advocate for yourself. This allows you to make choices that you understand and feel good about in the long run.

      I help mommas and their partners have the best birth experiences possible by offering support that adjusts to labor’s unpredictable nature. This model increases the opportunity for self-empowerment, informed decision making, and staying aligned with what each couple holds dear on their journey to parenthood.

    EXPECTATIONS

    • the visits

      Preliminary Visit: The doula and family relationship is just that, a relationship. So it is crucial that we feel like we're the right fit. This short visit is to discuss your expectations of a doula, and to see if we're a good match. We can meet in your home or a coffee shop, whichever space makes you most comfortable. I will give you my contract and invite you to take it home before making a decision.

      2 Prenatal Home Visits: We'll explore your birth, your hopes, and your fears. I'll bring out my rebozo and we'll practice positions to find what feels good. We will discuss signs of labor and when you would like me to join you. I'll also answer any questions you have about labor, including potential medical solutions to bring on labor or alleviate dicomfort.

      Labor and Birth: I'll give you both support and space in turn.

      Postpartum Home Visit: We get to see each other one more time! We will debrief your birth experience and celebrate your birth, your baby, and your growing family. I'll also address any concerns you have and give referrals as necessary.
